Prologue of the End

Prologue of the End is an opening essay on career uncertainty in science and biotech, written at a moment when I was genuinely unsure whether to keep going in R&D, pursue an MBA, or pivot entirely. I draw on Marc Andreessen's argument against rigid career planning to make the case that tolerance for ambiguity — not a clear roadmap — is the skill that actually compounds over time. The essay is candid in a way that face-to-face conversation rarely is: it names the uncomfortable in-between state of being early-career in a field moving faster than any plan could account for. It's the opening piece for what I intend to be a longer thread of writing on science, biotech, and the navigation of both.
